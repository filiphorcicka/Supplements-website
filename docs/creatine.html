How Creatine Works

Creatine is a naturally occurring compound found in muscle cells that helps produce energy during high-intensity exercise. Supplementation increases phosphocreatine stores in muscles, allowing for greater ATP production - the energy currency of cells.

Unlike many supplements with questionable efficacy, creatine has over 500 peer-reviewed studies demonstrating its safety and effectiveness.

ATP Regeneration - Helps replenish ATP faster during intense exercise

Performance - Most effective for short-duration, high-intensity activities

Cellular Hydration - Increases water content in muscle cells

Optimal Creatine Protocol

While creatine will eventually saturate your muscles with daily maintenance doses, a loading phase can accelerate the process to achieve benefits within days rather than weeks.

Loading Phase (5-7 days)

Take 20g of creatine monohydrate daily, divided into 4 doses of 5g each. This rapidly saturates muscle creatine stores.

Maintenance Phase (ongoing)

After loading, reduce to 3-5g daily to maintain elevated creatine stores. Timing isn't critical - consistency is key.

Hydration

Increase water intake as creatine draws water into muscle cells. Aim for at least 3L daily during loading phase.

Creatine FAQs

Common questions about creatine supplementation answered

Will creatine make me gain fat?

No, creatine does not cause fat gain. Any initial weight increase (typically 1-3kg) is from water being drawn into your muscles, not fat. Long-term, creatine may support fat loss by enabling more intense workouts.

Do I need to cycle creatine?

No cycling is necessary. Unlike some supplements, creatine can be taken continuously. Your muscles will simply maintain elevated creatine stores while supplementing and gradually return to baseline if you stop.

Which form of creatine is best?

Creatine monohydrate is the most researched, effective, and cost-efficient form. Other varieties (HCl, ethyl ester, etc.) offer no proven advantages despite marketing claims and often cost significantly more.

Should I take creatine on rest days?

Yes, maintaining consistent creatine levels is important. Your muscles don't "use up" creatine only on workout days - it's always present in your system supporting cellular energy processes.
